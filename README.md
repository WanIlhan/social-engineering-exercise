# Exercise 1: Phishing Emails in Action

## Introduction
Phishing emails are a common form of social engineering where attackers attempt to trick users into revealing sensitive information by pretending to be a trusted organization.

## Indicators of Phishing Emails
- Suspicious sender email address
- Urgent or threatening language
- Unexpected attachments or links
- Requests for personal information

## Example Scenario
An email claims to be from a bank and asks the user to verify their account by clicking a link.

## Impact
Phishing attacks can lead to financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

## Prevention and Awareness
- Always check the sender's email address
- Do not click unknown links
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Report suspicious emails

## Conclusion
Understanding how phishing emails work helps users avoid becoming victims of social engineering attacks.
